# PRD template

The shape of `docs/ideas/<slug>/prd.md`. Sections map one-to-one onto the
interview, so a section with nothing under it means a question that was never
answered — go back and ask it rather than writing filler.

Written for a reader who was not in the room and knows nothing about the
implementation. No stack, no schema, no API, no estimates.

```markdown
# <the one-sentence idea, as the person accepted it>

<Type: new product | new capability in <product>>

## The pain

Who, in what situation, and what it costs them. Concrete enough that a reader
recognises the person.

## How they cope today

The incumbent — a tool, a habit, a workaround, or enduring it. This is what the
idea has to beat.

## What we imagine

The idea in plain language: what someone would be able to do that they cannot
do now. Written as an experience, not a mechanism.

## Success looks like

One checkable sentence. The observable difference between the world with this
and the world without it.

## Deliberately not

What this leaves out, stated as a promise. Each line here is scope someone will
later try to add back.

## Riskiest assumption

The belief that carries the whole idea. If it is false, nothing below matters.
Name how it could be tested cheaply, if a way exists.

## Why here (new capability only)

What in the existing product makes it the natural home for this.

## Open questions

What nobody in the room knew. Left open on purpose.

## Assumptions

Gaps filled in by the writer rather than the person interviewed — flagged so a
reader never mistakes them for the idea's own claims.
```
